# Review Rubric

Use this rubric for the seven scored dimensions. Keep the output concise, but anchor every score to observable traits in the text.

## Global Scale

- `5.0`: Rare, editor-impressing quality.
- `4.0`: Professional and confidently shareable.
- `3.0`: Solid but ordinary.
- `2.0`: Noticeable problems reduce trust, clarity, or impact.
- `1.0`: Serious weakness or likely failure for the piece's purpose.
- `0.0`: Entirely absent or fundamentally broken.

When unsure, choose the lower score.

## Mode Lens

### Mode A: Information and Utility First

Prioritize logic, structure, evidence, practical usefulness, and reader guidance.

### Mode B: Emotion and Empathy First

Prioritize honesty, internal emotional coherence, voice, resonance, and scene or feeling construction.

## 1. Reliability and Persuasiveness

Focus:

- Mode A: logical consistency, evidence, assumptions, scope, counterarguments, factual support.
- Mode B: emotional honesty, lack of posturing, coherent inner movement, no borrowed or ornamental sincerity.

Anchors:

- `5.0`: Feels fully trustworthy and hard to dismiss.
- `4.0`: Clear support and solid framing, with only minor gaps.
- `3.0`: Mostly believable, but some claims or feelings feel under-supported.
- `2.0`: Assertions outrun support, or the emotional truth feels performative.
- `1.0`: Major credibility or integrity problems.

## 2. Structure and Storytelling

Focus:

- Mode A: conclusion-first structure, information hierarchy, non-overlapping sections, smooth progression.
- Mode B: narrative arc, scene transitions, tension and release, payoff, aftertaste.

Anchors:

- `5.0`: The structure carries the reader effortlessly.
- `4.0`: Strong flow with only small rough edges.
- `3.0`: Understandable but drifts, repeats, or sags.
- `2.0`: Feels scattered, list-like, or abruptly unfinished.
- `1.0`: No discernible organization or narrative thread.

## 3. Reader Gift

Focus:

- Mode A: practical value, reproducibility, problem-solving, time saved, new understanding.
- Mode B: empathy, relief, delight, perspective shift, catharsis.

Anchors:

- `5.0`: The reader clearly leaves with something substantial.
- `4.0`: Real value or resonance is present.
- `3.0`: Some value, but familiar or limited.
- `2.0`: Mostly self-expression without enough return for the reader.
- `1.0`: No identifiable value or takeaway for anyone but the author.

## 4. Expression and Readability

Focus:

- sentence rhythm
- word choice
- clarity of phrasing
- visual readability
- for Mode B, sensory detail and metaphor quality when relevant

Anchors:

- `5.0`: The language is exceptionally clear or vivid.
- `4.0`: Smooth, readable, and controlled.
- `3.0`: Serviceable but flat, repetitive, or uneven.
- `2.0`: Friction from clutter, awkward grammar, long walls of text, or weak phrasing.
- `1.0`: Nearly unreadable due to incoherent phrasing, extreme density, or pervasive errors.

## 5. Temperature and Distance

Focus:

- reader awareness
- humility and stance
- whether the reader feels guided, invited, or pushed away
- whether humor and intimacy are used with control

Anchors:

- `5.0`: The writer-reader relationship feels expertly managed.
- `4.0`: Reader care is visible and effective.
- `3.0`: Basically polite, but generic or distant.
- `2.0`: One-sided, preachy, or oblivious to reader needs.
- `1.0`: Actively hostile, condescending, or completely detached from the audience.

## 6. Social Risk and Harm Avoidance

This score is inverted from typical defect scoring: higher is safer.

Focus:

- stereotyping or overgeneralization
- disguised contempt or ridicule
- slurs, loaded phrasing, or unnecessarily inflammatory wording
- universalizing from narrow personal experience
- passages that become harmful when quoted out of context

Anchors:

- `5.0`: Careful, precise, and hard to weaponize unfairly.
- `4.0`: Generally safe, with only minor phrasing risk.
- `3.0`: Noticeable exposure to misreading or overreach.
- `2.0`: Material risk of backlash, harm, or unfairness.
- `1.0`: Serious publication risk.

If the score is below `3.0`, recommend reconsidering publication until revised.

## 7. Human Distinctiveness

Focus:

- specificity of lived experience
- idiosyncratic detail
- vulnerability, obsession, or earned conviction
- surprising but coherent connections

Anchors:

- `5.0`: Strongly singular voice or perspective.
- `4.0`: Clearly human and personal.
- `3.0`: Competent but generic.
- `2.0`: Interchangeable or heavily templated.
- `1.0`: Mechanically generic or obviously synthetic.

## Overall Score Guidance

- Do not average blindly.
- Let the overall score reflect what an editor would believe about publishability and impact after reading the whole piece.
- Strong safety or credibility issues should cap the overall score, even when the prose is stylish.
- A technically clean but generic piece should not score as highly as a distinctive piece that is equally clean and more memorable.
